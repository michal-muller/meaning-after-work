# Carrying the Stone v0.1 — Design Notes

Carrying the Stone is a visual reflective experience about work, AI, burden, agency and human meaning.

It is not a psychological test, diagnostic instrument or therapeutic tool.

## Core metaphor

The stone represents the burden created when work, usefulness and human worth become entangled.

The hill represents the moving demands of productivity, acceleration and self-optimization.

The player is not asked to win. The player is invited to notice how the stone changes.

## Dimensions

- Burden: the felt weight of pressure, usefulness and performance.
- Meaning: whether the climb remains connected to human purpose.
- Agency: whether the person can still choose, judge and redefine the task.
- Co-being: whether the burden is carried relationally rather than alone.

## Starting values

All dimensions start at 50.

Scores are bounded between 0 and 100.

## General scoring logic

- Silent adaptation increases Burden and lowers Meaning, Agency and Co-being.
- Total refusal may increase Agency but can lower Co-being or Responsibility-like orientation.
- Human checkpoints increase Meaning and Agency.
- Shared questioning increases Co-being and often lowers Burden.
- Redefining the climb increases Meaning and Agency.
- Asking who carries the burden increases Co-being.

## Result profiles

The current profiles are:

1. The Exhausted Climber  
   High Burden and low Meaning.

2. The Shared Carrier  
   High Co-being.

3. The Redefiner of the Climb  
   High Meaning and Agency.

4. The Solitary Rebel  
   High Agency but low Co-being.

5. The Careful Carrier  
   Default reflective profile.

## Data ethics

The current version stores nothing, sends nothing and uses no database.
All choices remain in the browser during the session only.
