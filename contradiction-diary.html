Reflective Experiences

Small games for serious reflection

These prototypes translate the ideas of Meaning After Work into short interactive experiences. They are not tests, diagnoses or productivity tools. They are invitations to notice how meaning, agency, responsibility and co-being change under pressure.

How to use these experiences

Use them in short moments of reflection: alone, in teaching, in workshops, or as prompts for conversation about AI, work and meaning.

They do not measure your worth. They help you notice patterns: where pressure rises, where agency disappears, where responsibility becomes unclear, and where co-being may return.

Before you begin

This page does not send, store or analyse your responses. Everything you write here stays in your browser unless you choose to copy it elsewhere.

Please do not enter names of other people, confidential workplace information, medical details or identifiable sensitive data. This is not therapy, diagnosis, legal advice or professional counselling.

What is an existential contradiction?

An existential contradiction is not simply a problem to be solved. It is a human situation in which different demands pull us in incompatible directions.

We may feel pulled between efficiency and dependency, care and exhaustion, responsibility and survival, creativity and skill erosion, productivity and meaning, or adaptation and self-betrayal.

The purpose of this diary is not to remove contradiction immediately. It is to help you name it, understand its pressures and ask what responsible agency might look like within it.
