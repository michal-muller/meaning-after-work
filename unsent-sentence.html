The Unsent Sentence

A short game about protecting a human sentence from becoming too efficient, too polished or too afraid.

Can a sentence stay human before it is optimized away?

Some sentences are never sent because they are too vulnerable, too unfinished or too difficult to make useful. This game gives you prepared fragments of an unsent sentence. Each choice changes what survives: honesty, warmth, courage, polish or pressure.

The goal is not to write the perfect message. The goal is to notice how easily a human sentence becomes professional, efficient, safe — and empty.

How to play

  • Each round gives you three possible sentence fragments.
  • Choose the fragment that you feel should survive.
  • The labels are hidden until after your choice.
  • The order of choices changes, so there is no obvious “correct” side.
  • If time runs out, the system chooses a safer fragment for you.

This prototype uses only prepared fragments. You do not need to type personal or sensitive text. It stores no data and sends nothing anywhere. The sentence is a metaphor for how easily human expression can become over-optimized, guarded or erased.

Meaning After Work is an independent public research-and-practice initiative. The Unsent Sentence is an experimental reflective game and not a diagnostic tool.
