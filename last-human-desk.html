Reflective game

The Last Human Desk

A short game about arranging work, AI, care and meaning on the last human desk.

Not everything on your desk belongs to the system.

Your desk fills with tasks, messages, AI recommendations, ethical concerns, unfinished conversations, tiredness, care and small signs of life.

Your task is not to clear everything. Your task is to decide what should be done, what can be delegated, what must remain human and what should be allowed to leave.

How to play

  • Click an item on the desk to select it.
  • Then choose: Do now, Delegate to AI, Keep human or Let go.
  • Some items are tasks. Some are people. Some are noise. Some are warnings.
  • The goal is not an empty desk. The goal is a humanly arranged desk.

Reflection

The Human Desk Survived

You delegated tasks, but kept judgment close.

This prototype is a reflective game, not a productivity assessment, psychological test, therapy, diagnosis or workplace assessment. It stores no data and sends nothing anywhere. The desk is a metaphor for how easily work, systems and care become mixed together.

Meaning After Work is an independent public research-and-practice initiative. The Last Human Desk is an experimental reflective game and not a diagnostic tool.
