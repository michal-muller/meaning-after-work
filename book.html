The book project

Meaning After Work

Staying Human When AI Transforms the Meaning of Work

A public-facing non-fiction book in development about artificial intelligence, meaningful work, human agency, responsibility and co-being.

The question behind the book

What happens to the meaning of life when the meaning of work begins to collapse?

For much of modern life, work has carried more than economic function. It has promised identity, usefulness, recognition, status, discipline, belonging and a way of understanding ourselves.

The rise of artificial intelligence unsettles this arrangement. AI does not merely automate tasks. It changes how people experience expertise, judgment, creativity, usefulness and value.

Meaning After Work asks what remains humanly meaningful when work can no longer carry all the identity, dignity and purpose that modern societies have placed upon it.

This is not a book against work or technology. It is a book about what remains human when intelligent systems reshape the work through which many people have understood who they are.

Why now

AI is not only changing what people do. It is changing what work means.

Public debate about artificial intelligence often focuses on productivity, risk, regulation, automation and skills. These questions matter. But a deeper question is emerging beneath them: what happens to human meaning when work, expertise and usefulness are reorganized by intelligent systems?

The AI age will not only require new tools, jobs and policies. It will require a renewed understanding of human presence, responsibility, judgment, care and co-being.

What the book explores

Core themes

Meaning

Work as a shelter of identity

How modern people have asked work to carry dignity, usefulness, status and purpose — and what happens when that shelter becomes unstable.

AI and usefulness

The crisis of being needed

How intelligent systems change the value of expertise, judgment, creativity and knowledge work.

Agency

Responsibility under pressure

How people and organizations can preserve moral agency when decisions are increasingly mediated by systems, dashboards and automation.

Co-being

Rediscovering relational life

Why the future of work is also a question of presence, care, dialogue, vulnerability and the human ability to remain with one another.

Leadership

Existential management

How leaders face contradiction, uncertainty, crisis and human vulnerability when technical solutions are not enough.

Practice

Reflective exercises

The book is being developed alongside essays and reflective experiences that help readers notice pressure, meaning, agency and responsibility in concrete situations.

Readers

Who the book is for

Leaders and managers

For people facing AI adoption who want to preserve judgment, responsibility, dignity and relational life inside organizations.

Professionals and workers

For readers who feel the tension between productivity, usefulness, identity, care and the need to remain human under pressure.

Researchers and educators

For those interested in AI, meaningful work, existential management, humanistic management and the future of work.

Public readers of serious non-fiction

For readers looking for a thoughtful book on technology, work, meaning, leadership and the human condition.
